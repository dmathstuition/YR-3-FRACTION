What it means
🟢 LIKE Denominators = Same bottom number!
💡
When the bottom numbers are the same, just add the top numbers.
The bottom number stays the same. Easy!
Example
🍕 Let's add: ²⁄₅ + ¹⁄₅

Think of a pizza cut into 5 equal slices!

25 + 15 = ?5

FRACTION BAR (out of 5 slices)

²⁄₅ + ¹⁄₅ = ³⁄₅ 🎉
We added 2 + 1 = 3 slices. The bottom stays 5!
The Simple Rule
📝 Formula
an + bn = a + bn
✏️
Add only the numerators (top).
Keep the denominator (bottom) the same.
What it means
🔵 UNLIKE Denominators = Different bottom numbers!
💡
When the bottom numbers are different, we must make them the same first.
We find the LCM (Lowest Common Multiple) and convert both fractions.
Example
🍫 Let's add: ¹⁄₂ + ¹⁄₃
Step 1
Write the fractions: ¹⁄₂ and ¹⁄₃
Step 2
Find LCM of 2 and 3 → LCM = 6
Multiples of 2: 2, 4, 6, 8…
Multiples of 3: 3, 6, 9…
Step 3
Convert both to sixths
12 × 33 = 36 | 13 × 22 = 26
Step 4
Now add! Same denominator ✅
36 + 26 = ?

FRACTION BAR (out of 6 equal parts)

¹⁄₂ + ¹⁄₃ = ⁵⁄₆ 🎉
3 + 2 = 5 sixths. That's ⁵⁄₆!
The Simple Rule
📝 Steps to Remember
1️⃣
Find the LCM of both denominators
2️⃣
Convert both fractions to the same denominator
3️⃣
Add the numerators — keep the new denominator!
